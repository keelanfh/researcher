I am a university student who enjoys applying mathematical and computational tools to real-world social challenges. I am particularly interested in the movement of people in cities. I also like maps and visualisations of data in all their forms.

I'm a second year student of Arts and Sciences, a Liberal Arts programme at University College London. The modules I've taken include Quantum Physics, Programming, Philosophy, Social Theory and Mathematics.

As part of my degree, I've recently completed a group project, The Electionary, analysing the use of language in American Presidential Election campaigns from 1960 up to the present day. I've analysed my walking behaviour to and from UCL to see which parts of my journey are faster and slower. I've written a quiz application for Android.

I'm also part of the Transport for London Youth Panel, an advisory group which discusses transport issues which affect young people. I'm also part of a similar group which works with Transport for West Midlands. I recently took part in a research project investigating students' experiences of the Bloomsbury campus to inform the plans for development at UCL East.

I relax by singing, and have sung with UCLU Symphony Chorus.

Contact me on Twitter, LinkedIn or GitHub, or email me at any address at this domain.

Geographically, I'm in the UK, between London and Birmingham.
